Executive Summary

The Upwork automation market showed signs of stabilization in October 2025, declining just 0.8% to 5,031 jobs after September's sharp -6.1% drop. From July's peak of 5,522 jobs, the market has declined 8.9% total, but the pace of contraction has slowed dramatically. Make.com and Notion showed strong rebounds (+6.2% and +27.4% respectively), while platform leaders continue gradual declines. Average hourly rates dropped to $38.17/hr, the first significant rate compression since May, suggesting market pressure on pricing.

Critical October Developments:

  • Slowing Decline: -0.8% in October vs -6.1% in September (92% improvement)
  • Platform Divergence: Make.com +6.2%, Notion +27.4% rebound; Zapier -4.5%, n8n -3.6%
  • Rate Compression Begins: $38.17/hr average, down from $38.74/hr (first meaningful decline)
  • Stabilization Pattern: All platforms declining 3-5% suggests new equilibrium forming
  • Application Recovery: Several apps rebounding after September crash
  • Market Maturation: Transition from rapid growth to stable, mature market
